Privacy Policy

Effective July 19, 2026 · Developed by Paulo Ruberto

Overview

Opus helps you follow music artists and browse their releases. Opus does not operate user accounts, a developer-operated backend, advertising, or analytics. Most Opus data stays locally on your device.

Information stored on your device

Opus stores information needed to provide its features locally on your device. This may include:

  • Artists you choose to follow
  • Release and album metadata retrieved for those artists
  • App settings, filters, and playback preferences
  • Apple Music and notification permission status
  • Cached artwork and related app data

Paulo Ruberto does not operate a server that receives or stores this Opus data.

Apple Music and music-content services

Opus uses Apple Music, MusicKit, and Apple-operated public music-content services to search for artists, retrieve release and artwork information, provide previews or playback, and, if you choose, add music to your Apple Music library.

When you use these features, search terms and the artist, album, or track identifiers needed to provide the requested feature are sent directly to Apple or its music-content services. Apple’s handling of information is governed by the Apple Privacy Policy .

If you grant Apple Music permission, Opus may access only the Apple Music information needed for the feature you use, such as checking whether a release is in your library or adding an item at your request. Opus does not receive or store your Apple Account password.

Notifications

With your permission, Opus may schedule local notifications on your device about newly discovered releases. Notification permission is optional and can be changed at any time in iOS Settings.

Opus does not send push notifications through a developer-operated server.

Data sharing, advertising, and tracking

Opus does not sell, rent, or share your personal information with advertisers, data brokers, or analytics providers. Opus does not use advertising or cross-app tracking.

Information necessary for Apple Music and music-content requests is sent to Apple as described above. Opus does not otherwise disclose your locally stored app data to third parties.

Data retention and deletion

Opus retains local app data until you remove it. You can delete Opus data using the app’s reset option in Settings, or by removing the app from your device. You can change Apple Music and notification permissions at any time in iOS Settings.

Because Opus does not use a developer-operated account system or server, there is no developer-held account data for us to retrieve or delete.

Children’s privacy

Opus is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if Opus’s data practices change. Any update will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
